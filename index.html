About

Matyáš Pávek · MTB Racer

My name is Matyáš Pávek, soon turning 22 in October 2026, and for those who don't know me yet, let me introduce myself as a lifelong lover of pretty much every sport you can imagine.

Since childhood, I've played tennis, tried gymnastics, football, and many other activities. Very early, I switched to what truly captured my heart — bikes, skis, and snowboards. Because I had to divide my time between summer and winter seasons, I eventually chose cross‑country skiing as my main winter sport… even though I somehow managed to become a youth national champion in snowboarding along the way.

In the summer, I stayed loyal to mountain bikes, mixed with classic road cycling. With this combo, I became a junior national MTB champion, competed at the European Championships, and even raced in the World Cup in Nové Město. I raced for Kooperativa Jablonec and later for Brilon Team Mladá Boleslav in Czechia.

In cross‑country skiing, I also became a national youth and later junior champion, racing for Dukla Liberec, with several starts at World Championships.

Matyáš Pávek · XC Ski Racer · University of Alaska Nanooks

Today, my main sport is still cross‑country skiing, and I proudly compete for the University of Alaska Nanooks in the USA, where I'm also a dedicated student. I could probably still compete for the Czech national team — but I chose the adventure of studying and racing in Alaska, and I'm loving every minute of it.

Even though skiing is my top sport now, bikes and gear remain a huge part of my heart and mind, and they'll stay with me for life. Once a gear‑head, always a gear‑head.

You can also meet me on a free downhill slope with my wide skis, or in summer on my enduro bike, where I sometimes end up racing my friends and my brother (and yes, bragging rights are very important). Climbing rock walls or going on long trips into beautiful mountains is also very much my cup of tea.

My goal is to travel 4,000 km as part of a personal challenge and preparation for the 2027 Africa Crossing, and I'm inviting you to be part of this journey.
